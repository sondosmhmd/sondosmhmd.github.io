The Opel Mokka is a subcompact crossover SUV that has been produced by German automaker Opel since 2012 .


Sales began with the model year of 2013 ,at the end of 2012.


The first generation was developed by GM Korea as the U200 Chevrolet Trax .

The facelifted Opel Mokka was marketed as the Mokka X, before reverting to Mokka for the second generation. It is also sold under the Vauxhall brand in the United Kingdom. The Buick Encore sold in North America and in China was also derived from the Trax but the lines diverged after Opel became part of Stellantis.

Opel Mokka 2020


This generation of Opel Mokka is being produced from January, 2020. The car is available only with front wheel drive. This Opel is available with two petrol engines with displacement 1.2 litres and power of from 100 to 130 horsepower un one diesel engine with displacement 1.5 litres and power of 110 HP un one electrical engine with power of 136 HP.


Opel Mokka 2020 average fuel consumption in combined cycle for petrol engine is from 4.5 to 4.8 litres per 100km, but for diesel engine - approximately 3.8 litres per 100km. The most fuel efficient Opel Mokka 2020 petrol engine version is Opel Mokka 1.2 Turbo, consuming 4.5 litres per 100km



Opel Mokka 2020 average energy consumption in combined cycle is from 16 to 17.6 kWh per 100km. The most economical Opel Mokka version is Opel Mokka Mokka-e 50 kWh, consuming 16 kilowatt hours of electricity per 100km .



The best acceleration from 0 to 100 km/h - 9 seconds has Opel Mokka Mokka-e 50 kWh version, conversely the tardiest one is Opel Mokka 1.5 Diesel reaching this speed only in 10.8 seconds. The maximum top speed is 202 km/h for the 1.2 petrol 3-cylinder 130 hp version (Opel Mokka 1.2 Turbo).



Lenght of Opel Mokka is 415 cm, it is medium size car. Opel Mokka gross weight is from 1700 to 2015 kg (depending on modification), this car is about 6 percent heavier than the average for this age and class of vehicles
